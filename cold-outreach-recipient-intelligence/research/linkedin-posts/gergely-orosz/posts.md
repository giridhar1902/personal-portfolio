This is how you wreck personal relationships with AI:

I met a tech enterpreneur in a cafe a few months back. A nice person. We talked about a few things, and said we'll keep in touch. Left with a good impression.

I get an email from him today. I open it with positivity... it evaporates after the first few sentences.

The email is AI slop, unmistakably. "It's not X, it's Y." "X is the clearest example.' Filler adjectives everywhere. "The interesting part of the story is..." and I can go on. 7 paragraphs of this AI-infused garbage.

The thing was a pitch and if it came from the person I met, I would have probably considered it, because I try to prioritize personal relationships.

But I now know this person outsources their thinking to AI, and would rather turn a two-sentence short email into a 7-paragraph AI-generated pitch.

The personal connection is dead, and I cannot see myself interacting with this person again. Because they decided to put an AI layer between the two of us, and I now will assume that AI responses are the most I can get from this person.

Learn from this person's mistake before you wreck your own personal relationships by reaching for AI to do what this person did. Sending an AI-generated message from someone you personally know is the equivalent of showing a middle finger to them. You are making it crystal clear that they are not worth your time to type out even a single sentence - it was just delegated to AI. (And if it was "AI-enhanced" turned into AI slop you prove you never bothered to read the output, else you would have never sent something sounding like such, and nothing of what you would have written - which proves the original point.)
