I’ve seen some people suggesting that instead of warm intros you should just send a cold email and reference the relationship. This is terrible advice.

First, whats the reasoning behind this suggestion?

It’s usually some form of “well warm intros are hard. They take a lot of work and they don’t always work.”

Yes, they take work, but this is coming from an industry that sees no issue with 100s of cold calls and 10s of thousands of emails per rep per week.

Things worth doing are often not the easiest things to do (though ya know, Commsor 🦕 can help you make your warm intro process significantly less painful and more effective 😉)

“Yeah but they don’t always work”

Uh yeah, welcome to sales?

People will celebrate booking 3 meetings from a week of cold calling, or 1000 cold emails, but balk at having to make 5 intro requests to get those same 3 meetings.

Back to the idea of skipping intros and just ‘referencing’ the relationship in your outbound.

Like everything, there are absolutely places where this could work, but the advice is terrible.

The reason why warm intros and referrals work is because of the transfer of trust they create.

Simply referencing the relationship doesn’t transfer that same trust.

It puts you in danger of burning the bridge with that relationship in the first place.

If you’re going to embrace your network and relatioships to sell - you better do it with integrity and authenticity.
